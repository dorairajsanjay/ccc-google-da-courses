AI Tools

Google AI Tools for Teaching and Learning

Available to all 116 colleges at no cost through the statewide MoU.

Gemini

Google's AI assistant for research, writing, analysis, coding, and creative tasks. Use it to explore concepts, analyze data, draft content, and support teaching workflows.

NotebookLM

AI-powered research and note-taking tool. Upload syllabi, articles, and reports — NotebookLM provides summaries, answers questions, and generates study guides grounded in your sources.

Google Workspace

Cloud-based productivity and collaboration suite including Docs, Sheets, Slides, Drive, and Meet. The Fundamentals edition is free for all educational institutions.

All tools are FERPA and COPPA compliant. Institutional data is not used for advertising or AI model training. Canvas LMS integration is supported via LTI. For implementation guidance, visit the Digital Center's Google for Education page.

Community

Faculty Community of Practice

Connect with faculty across the state who are integrating Google content into their programs.

The community of practice is designed for low time commitment while providing meaningful peer support. It includes:

  • Async communication — Slack/Discord channels for questions, resource sharing, and collaboration
  • Quarterly meetings — Structured sessions covering implementation challenges, best practices, and new developments
  • Professional development credit — Participation counts toward PD requirements at participating colleges
  • Shared resources — Templates, implementation guides, assessment rubrics, and Canvas course materials

Priority Training Topics

Based on the January 2026 faculty focus group:

  • Assessment strategies for certificate content (88%)
  • LTI / Canvas integration setup (75%)
  • Coursera platform navigation (63%)

63% of focus group faculty were unfamiliar with Google Data Analytics content prior to the session — hands-on training matters.
