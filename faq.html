Guidance for colleges, faculty, and administrators on accessing and implementing Google Career Certificates across the California Community Colleges.

Access and Eligibility

Nine Career Certificates: IT Support, IT Automation with Python, Cybersecurity, Project Management, Data Analytics, Advanced Data Analytics, Business Intelligence, UX Design, and Digital Marketing & E-Commerce.

Ten Professional Specializations: AI Essentials, Prompting Essentials, Accelerate Your Job Search with AI, Data Analysis with Python, Data-Driven Decision Making, Digital Customer Engagement, Network Security, People Management, Stakeholder Management, and Agile Essentials.

AI Training for Faculty: Getting Started with Google AI in Higher Education, Generative AI for Educators, Teaching Responsible Use of AI, and Gemini Academy.

No. The MoU is non-binding and involves no exchange of funds. Google Career Certificates, specializations, and Coursera scholarships are provided at no cost through the Chancellor's Office. There are no licensing fees, subscription costs, or per-student charges.

The MoU is effective in perpetuity unless terminated by either party with 30 days' written notice. Coursera course access is renewed on a 1-year cycle. Google Workspace tools (Gemini, NotebookLM) are perpetual.

  1. Complete Google's institutional access form to receive free Coursera scholarships.
  2. Create a free Coursera for Campus account using the same email address.
  3. Add additional administrators as needed to manage the program.

Each institution defines its own process for distributing access to faculty and students. The Chancellor's Office can support faculty onboarding.

All 116 CCC colleges are covered under the statewide MoU. However, individual districts may need to complete a separate agreement with Google for local implementation of tools. Contact the Chancellor's Office for guidance on your district's status.

Certificate Structure and Integration

Google Data Analytics Certificate: ~189 hours across 8 courses plus an AI Job Search module.

Google Advanced Data Analytics Certificate: ~205 hours across 7 courses.

Specializations are shorter — AI Essentials is approximately 5 hours, Prompting Essentials approximately 6 hours.

The January 2026 faculty focus group identified five packaging options:

  • Option A: Full Certificate Program (12 units) — Four courses at 3 units each. Financial aid eligible. Requires curriculum committee approval.
  • Option B: Embedded Modules — Integrate Google content into existing approved courses. No new curriculum approval needed. Fastest path for credit-bearing courses.
  • Option C: Stackable Micro-credentials — 2-4 unit pieces that stack toward a full certificate. Flexible for working adults.
  • Option D: Non-Credit / Continuing Education — No curriculum committee approval required. Fastest to implement. Fundable through Strong Workforce.
  • Option E: Hybrid Credit + Google Certificate — Students earn both CCC credit and the Google industry credential simultaneously.

Faculty strongly favored hybrid packaging (88%) and embedded modules (88%) over creating entirely new standalone courses (25%).

Credit and CPL (Credit for Prior Learning)

Yes. Colleges can award Credit for Prior Learning (CPL) to students who complete Google Career Certificates. CPL registration with MAP (Multiple Award Pathway) is in progress at the CCC state level. Learn more about CPL pathways →

Faculty from the January 2026 focus group preferred:

  • Combination of portfolio review + exam (63%)
  • Portfolio review alone (38%)
  • Challenge exam (38%)
  • Direct articulation (25%)
  • Faculty interview (25%)

Contact Samuel Lee at the Chancellor's Office (slee@cccco.edu) for systemwide CPL questions. SJCC is currently registering its Data Analytics Certificate program with CPL and MAP, which may serve as a reference for other colleges.

Canvas and LMS Integration

Yes — 19 ready-to-use Canvas course shells for the Data Analytics pathway are available on Canvas Commons under a CC BY 4.0 license. Each includes capstone project guidelines, Coursera integration instructions, faculty customization guides, assignment rubrics, and student orientation materials. Browse courses →

Coursera content can be integrated into Canvas via LTI (Learning Tools Interoperability). Known technical considerations:

  • Login/authentication: 88% of focus group faculty anticipated challenges, particularly at colleges using Microsoft environments
  • LTI integration: 75% anticipated potential configuration issues
  • Grade sync: Grade transfer from Coursera to Canvas is currently manual. Automated grade sync is a goal for future integration work.

Colleges should work with their IT teams and the Chancellor's Office to test Coursera-Canvas integration before full deployment.

Faculty Training and Support

  • Google training resources: Gemini Academy, Getting Started with Google AI in Higher Education, Generative AI for Educators, and Teaching Responsible Use of AI
  • Faculty community of practice: Async communication via Slack/Discord plus quarterly meetings
  • Implementation materials: Canvas shell template, playbooks, and Creative Commons course materials
  • Google office hours: Regular sessions for technical and implementation questions

Focus group participants recommended 3-5 hours combining video modules and live sessions. Priority topics:

  • Assessment strategies for certificate content (88%)
  • LTI / Canvas integration setup (75%)
  • Coursera platform navigation (63%)

63% of focus group faculty were unfamiliar or only somewhat familiar with Google Data Analytics content prior to the session, indicating that hands-on training is important for adoption.

Assessment and Student Outcomes

  • Industry-relevant deliverables as the primary assessment method (100% agreement)
  • Authentic real-world projects reflecting actual workplace tasks (75%)
  • Skills-based verification demonstrating practical competency (88%)

Traditional exams were deprioritized, as faculty noted AI tools make look-up-style questions trivial.

  • Data visualization / dashboard (100%)
  • Problem definition and scoping (88%)
  • Data cleaning and preparation (88%)
  • Presentation to an audience (75%)

Data Privacy and Compliance

Google Workspace Core Services (including Gemini and NotebookLM) comply with FERPA and COPPA. For Coursera specifically, faculty raised data privacy concerns during the focus group that are being addressed before systemwide rollout. Colleges should review their own data sharing agreements with Coursera.

The MoU requires 30 days' written notice for termination. As a risk mitigation measure, colleges are encouraged to archive course content locally where possible. The rollout plan includes contingency planning for content continuity.
