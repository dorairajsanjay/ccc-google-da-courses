AI Entrepreneurship & Innovation

Building a statewide ecosystem where CCC students, faculty, and staff can create, incubate, and scale AI-powered solutions — transforming learners from consumers of technology into builders and founders.

What This Initiative Is

The AI Entrepreneurship, Apprenticeship, and Innovation Initiative cultivates AI innovation and entrepreneurial capacity across the California Community Colleges. Managed in partnership with Workforce Innovation Labs (WIL) and supported by the Foundation for California Community Colleges, this initiative provides infrastructure, mentorship, and resources for community college communities to design, develop, and launch AI tools.

The initiative encompasses three active projects — the CCC AI Innovation Marketplace, the Theuth AI Writing Assistant, and the Makerspace AI Assistant — each developed by cross-college teams of faculty, students, and industry partners.

How It Supports the System

  • Turning students into builders. Structured pathways for students to design, develop, and deploy AI tools — building portfolios, earning stipends, and gaining apprenticeship-level experience.
  • Solving real problems. Each project addresses a concrete need identified by colleges — an AI writing assistant, a virtual makerspace coach, and a collaboration platform.
  • Innovation-to-adoption pipeline. Through the AI Marketplace and an umbrella incubator hosted by the Foundation for CCC, promising projects receive fiscal sponsorship, IP protection, and a pathway to systemwide adoption.

Active Projects

Three Projects in Development

CCC AI Innovation Marketplace

A digital platform where students, faculty, and industry partners post projects, form teams, collaborate, and move innovations from concept to adoption. Includes built-in IP protection through e-signature NDAs, default ownership agreements, and an umbrella incubation pathway through the Foundation for CCC.

Status: Initial prototype built. MVP targeted for launch ahead of the September 2026 CCC Futures Summit with 5-10 active pilot projects.

Theuth AI Writing Assistant

An AI writing assistant built on the SPARK pedagogical framework — students Start with their own ideas, Partner with AI, Analyze contributions critically, Refine through synthesis, and Keep their authentic voice. Unlike general-purpose AI tools, Theuth never provides completed thesis statements or copy-paste language. It uses Socratic prompting to guide students while preserving academic integrity.

Faculty can configure Theuth with their rubrics, assignments, and course materials. Integrates with Canvas via LTI 1.3. Phase 1 complete; Phase 2 (enhanced AI interactions, challenge-based learning) underway.

Makerspace AI Assistant

A virtual coach for college makerspaces — supports onboarding, project planning, machine setup, real-time troubleshooting, and safety enforcement. Customized to each makerspace's specific equipment manuals, SOPs, and policies. Safety-first: enforces PPE requirements, gates access based on certification, and escalates to human staff when needed.

Partners: MAKERUSA, Cabrillo College, Krause Innovation Center. Product requirements complete; active development underway.
