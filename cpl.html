What is CPL?

Credit for Prior Learning (CPL) is a process that allows students to earn college credit by demonstrating knowledge and skills they've already acquired — through work experience, military training, industry certifications, or programs like Google Career Certificates on Coursera.

If you've already completed Google's Data Analytics Certificate, you shouldn't have to sit through courses covering material you already know. CPL gives you a fast path to college credit through skills assessment alone.

You've already done the work — now get the credit.

Key Facts

  • No Coursera coursework required — you won't retake courses
  • Assessment-only — 100% of your grade comes from skills verification
  • C or better to pass — standard passing threshold
  • Real college credit — counts toward certificates and degrees
  • Available now — 9 CPL courses on Canvas Commons

The Process

How CPL Works

Three steps from existing certificate to college credit.

1

Show Your Certificate

Present your Google Career Certificate (Coursera completion) as evidence of prior learning. This demonstrates you've already mastered the core content.

2

Complete Skills Verification

Take a faculty-designed skills assessment to verify your competency. 100% of your grade is based on these practical assessments — not exams or busy work.

3

Earn College Credit

Pass with a C or better and receive community college credit on your transcript. Credits count toward certificates, degrees, and transfer pathways.
